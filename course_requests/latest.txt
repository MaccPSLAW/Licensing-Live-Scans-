ABOUT MACCESS INC.
MACCESS INC. is a California-based provider of professional
security guard training and licensing support. We deliver
BSIS-compliant instruction that prepares security professionals
for safe, lawful, and confident performance in the field,
combining clear legal grounding with practical, real-world
application.

COURSE OVERVIEW
The Power to Arrest and Appropriate Use of Force training is
mandatory for all individuals seeking a California BSIS Security
Guard registration (guard card). It must be completed before a
security guard begins employment. Students must take and pass
the BSIS examination with a score of 100% to successfully
complete the training.

WHO THIS COURSE IS FOR
- New applicants pursuing a California BSIS guard card
- Security professionals refreshing required PTA/AUOF knowledge
- Employers seeking compliant, well-trained personnel

================================================================
PART 1 - POWER TO ARREST COURSE OUTLINE (3 HOURS)
================================================================
Objective: To familiarize and instruct the individual on the
training topics in Business & Professions Code 7583.7, including
legal aspects, techniques, liability, and company requirements
relating to the arrest of an individual.

1. Overview of Power to Arrest and Appropriate Use of Force
   Training Manual and subject matter
2. Responsibilities and ethics in citizen arrest and types of
   encounters
3. Relationship between security personnel and a peace officer
   in making an arrest
4. Limitations on security personnel power to arrest, including:
   a. Background on private security industry
   b. Contractual obligations
   c. Company policies
5. Restrictions on searches and seizures
6. Criminal and civil liabilities, including:
   a. Personal liability
   b. Employer liability
7. Trespass law
8. Ethics and communications
9. Emergency situation response, including response to medical
   emergencies
10. Security officer safety

================================================================
PART 2 - APPROPRIATE USE OF FORCE COURSE OUTLINE (5 HOURS)
================================================================
Objective: To introduce, instruct, and familiarize the
individual on the appropriate use of force topics in Business &
Professions Code 7583.7. This portion is delivered in-person
through traditional classroom instruction.

1. Legal standards for use of force
   a. Statutes
   b. Licensee and client contractual obligations
   c. Civil and criminal liability
2. The use of objectively reasonable force (in-person)
   a. Objectively reasonable standard
   b. Restraint techniques and their implications
   c. Force options
   d. Real-life scenarios
3. Duty to intercede
4. Supervisory responsibilities
   a. Incident reporting requirements pursuant to 7583.2,
      7583.4, and 7574.37 as applicable
5. Use of force review and analysis (in-person)
   a. Real-life scenarios
6. De-escalation and interpersonal communication training,
   including tactical methods using time, distance, cover, and
   concealment (in-person)
   a. Common misconceptions and benefits of de-escalation
   b. Four concepts of de-escalation: self-control, effective
      communication, scene assessment and management, force
      options
   c. Real-life scenarios
7. Implicit and explicit bias and cultural competency
   a. Define and explain: implicit bias, explicit bias,
      cultural competency
   b. Strategies for effective communication within a diverse
      community
   c. Real-life scenarios
8. Interacting with people with disabilities or behavioral
   health issues
   a. Strategies for de-escalating and communicating
   b. Real-life scenarios
9. Use of force scenario training, including simulations of
   low-frequency/high-risk situations and shoot/don't-shoot
   decision-making (in-person)
   a. Factors affecting response when threatened with danger
   b. Factors to consider before using force
   c. Real-life scenarios
10. Mental health and policing, including bias and stigma
   a. Categories of mental illness per Section 631
   b. Biases and stigmas surrounding mental illness
   c. Real-life scenarios
11. Active shooter situations
   a. Recognizing an active shooter situation
   b. Roles and responsibilities of security personnel
   c. Real-life scenarios

EXAMINATION & CERTIFICATION
================================================================
Individuals required to take the Power to Arrest and Appropriate
Use of Force training shall take and pass an examination as
provided in the BSIS Manual with a score of 100% in order to
successfully complete the training. Upon successful completion,
MACCESS INC. issues a Certificate of Completion that meets the
appearance requirements stated in Title 16, California Code of
Regulations, Section 643(b).
